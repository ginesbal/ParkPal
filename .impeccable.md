## Design Context

### Users
Drivers, commuters, and downtown visitors in Calgary who need to find parking quickly while already on the move. Most interactions are glanceable, time-sensitive, and happen one-handed on a phone.

### Brand Personality
Calm, dependable, and street-smart. The interface should feel polished and modern, but still practical enough for a utility app people trust when they are under time pressure.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light-mode first, map-forward, and utility-led. Keep the existing product structure, but refine it with cooler tinted neutrals, one primary blue accent, restrained warning color, tactile mobile controls, and clearer hierarchy. Avoid flashy gradients, novelty patterns, or a marketing-style redesign.

### Design Principles
- Prioritize glanceable decisions over decorative detail.
- Use one primary accent consistently and reserve warm color for warnings or urgency.
- Make touch interactions feel confident with clear pressed, selected, and focused states.
- Design empty, loading, and fallback states to teach the next best action.
- Keep the interface spatially clean and consistent across list, map, and session flows.
